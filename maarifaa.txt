Student Visa Application
Steps:
1.Get Admission Confirmation – Obtain an official
admission letter from an Indian university.
2.Prepare Required Documents:
Passport with at least six months validity.
Completed visa application form.
Admission letter from the university.
Proof of financial means to support education
and stay.
Recent passport-size photographs.
Medical certificate (if required).
3.Apply for a Visa:
Visit the nearest Indian embassy or apply online
via the Indian Visa Application Portal.
Pay the student visa fee (varies by country).
4.Attend Visa Interview (if required): Some Indian
embassies may request an interview before issuing a
visa.
5.Receive the Visa: Processing time is typically 2–4
weeks.
Foreigners Regional
Registration Office
(FRRO) Guide
What is FRRO?
The FRRO is responsible for the registration, visa
extension, and residence permits of foreign nationals in
India.
FRRO Registration Process for International Students:
Who Needs to Register? 
Any student staying in India for more than 180 days.
When to Register? 
Within 14 days of arrival in India.
How to Register?
Visit the FRRO Online Portal at
https://indianfrro.gov.in/
Upload scanned copies of required documents:
Passport and Visa Copy
University Admission Letter
Residential Address Proof (Hostel/PG Rental
Agreement)
Passport-Size Photographs
Pay the FRRO processing fee (if applicable).
Attend an in-person verification if required.
Failure to register with FRRO can result in penalties or
visa cancellation.
Pre-Arrival
Preparations
Essential Pre-Arrival
Checklist
Ensure you have a valid student visa and passport. 
Book flight tickets in advance. 
Arrange for accommodation (University Hostel, PG,
or Rental Apartment). 
Get a health check-up and necessary vaccinations. 
Carry original academic documents and admission
letter. 
Exchange currency or arrange an international
debit/credit card. 
Download essential mobile apps for navigation,
banking, and communication. 
Learn about Indian culture, food, and climate to ease
adaptation.
What to Pack?
Weather-appropriate clothing (India has varying
climates across regions). 
Prescription medicines and a medical history report.
Basic utensils and cultural snacks for comfort. 
Power adapter (India uses Type D & M plugs, 230V).
Life in India:
What to Expect?
Accommodation & Living Costs
University hostels: Affordable and safe for students.
PGs & Shared Apartments: Common in metro cities
like Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore.
Monthly living expenses: $150 – $500 (varies by
city).
Food & Transportation
Indian cuisine is diverse; international food options
are widely available.
Affordable transport: Metro, buses, auto-rickshaws,
and Uber/Ola taxis.
Students can get transport passes for metros and
buses.
Banking & Money Management
Open an Indian bank account (SBI, ICICI, HDFC,
etc.).
UPI-based payments (Google Pay, PhonePe) are
widely used.
Student Culture & Safety
Many universities have international student
associations.
Indian people are generally welcoming, and safety
measures exist on campuses.
Emergency helplines and local police contacts
should be noted.
